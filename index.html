Linux Distros for Beginners

A simple guide to get started with Linux

What is Linux?

Linux is an operating system, just like Windows or macOS. An OS is the main software that runs your computer and manages how programs and hardware work together. But what makes Linux special is that it's free, open-source and community-driven. That means anyone can download it, use it or even cange it to suit their needs.

Instead of being owned by one company, Linux is built and improved by thousands of people around the world, and there are many versions of linux called distributions or distros for short, such as Linux Mint, or Zorin OS. Each distro has its own pupose and style, some are designed super easy for begginers, others focus on programming or gaming, etc.

Because Linux is so flexible and customisable, you can find it everywhere, from laptops to cars, and here we will help you choose the righ distro for your needs.

Popular Beginner-Friendly Distros

Ubuntu

Very user-friendly, with great community support and easy installation.

Linux Mint

Ideal for users switching from Windows — clean interface and stability.

Zorin OS

Looks and feels like Windows, perfect for first-time Linux users.

CachyOS

Good for personalisation and gaming, it uses Arch Linux.
