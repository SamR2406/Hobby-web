Desktop Personalisation

Linux gives you the freedom to change almost everything about your desktop — from icons and animations to panels and shortcuts. Here are four popular environments so you can choose and edit your own.

Hyprland

Hyprland is a modern Wayland compositor known for its smooth animations and advanced window-tiling. It’s lightweight, fast, and highly configurable using text files. You can change animations, window gaps, themes, and even per-app behaviour. It’s perfect if you love minimalism and keyboard control.

KDE Plasma

KDE Plasma is one of the most customisable desktops available. You can move panels, change window borders, add widgets, and switch global themes easily. It’s visually rich and great for users who want a balance between beauty and flexibility without touching code.

Cinnamon

Cinnamon is the default desktop for Linux Mint, designed for simplicity and comfort. It offers a familiar layout similar to Windows, with a panel, start menu, and system tray. You can easily switch themes, icon packs, and add applets or desklets to make it truly yours.

Gnome

GNOME focuses on simplicity and productivity with a clean, minimal interface. It’s the default desktop for Ubuntu and Fedora, designed to stay out of your way while you work. Personalisation is less about widgets and more about extensions — small add-ons that change how GNOME looks and behaves.
